# The T-Shaped Product Professional

**Source:** Roman Pichler — [The T-Shaped Product Professional](https://www.romanpichler.com/blog/the-t-shaped-product-manager/) (2017)

---

## Summary

Product managers need two skill sets simultaneously: deep product-specific knowledge (vertical bar) and broad generic product management capabilities (horizontal bar). This T-shape allows them to work methodically across different products and organisations while still having the depth to make the right decisions for their specific product.

---

## The Two Dimensions

### Horizontal: Generic Product Management Skills
Transferable across products, markets, and companies. Divided into three groups:

| Skill group | What it covers |
|---|---|
| **Strategic** | Product strategy, roadmapping, business model design |
| **Tactical** | Requirements capture, backlog management, idea validation |
| **Leadership** | Guiding dev teams, inspiring stakeholders, reaching agreements |

All three are required — even tactical PMs benefit from strengthening strategy and leadership skills (career mobility, better collaboration with strategic roles).

### Vertical: Product-Specific Skills
Deep knowledge tied to a specific product, line, or portfolio:
- User needs and behaviour
- Competitive landscape and market trends
- The product's own value proposition, features, and KPIs
- How the company works — its goals, processes, and influencers

---

## Developing the T-Shape

**Horizontal growth:**
- Audit skills across strategy, tactics, and leadership
- Focus first on what helps most with the current job
- Pursue the strategically weakest area to unlock career mobility

**Vertical growth:**
- Talk to users at least quarterly (no substitute for real contact)
- Analyse feedback data and product usage regularly
- Compete-watch: review competitor products for differentiation signals
- Build relationships with dev team, Scrum Master, and key stakeholders
- Stay connected to company strategy via internal communications

---

## Key Takeaways

- Deep product skills alone are **not enough** — generic capability is what makes PMs employable, mobile, and effective across contexts
- Strategic skills in particular are underinvested — most PMs skew tactical
- T-shape is also a career ladder: mastering horizontal skills enables the move to Level 2 and Level 3 [[empowerment-levels]]
- Vertical skills decay if not actively maintained through user contact and market monitoring
