# Empowerment Levels in Product Management

**Source:** Roman Pichler — [Understanding Empowerment in Product Management](https://www.romanpichler.com/blog/empowerment-levels-in-product-management/) (2024)

---

## Summary

Three levels of decision-making authority describe how empowered a product person actually is. The model maps authority to the three domains of product work: delivery, discovery, and strategy.

---

## The Three Levels

### Level 1: Product Delivery
*Authority to decide how features are implemented*

Signs:
- Stakeholders or management determine which features to build
- Product backlog / user stories / Jira tickets are the main artefacts
- Cannot decline feature requests

Verdict: **Not sufficient** for effective product management. Role is closer to delivery or project manager.

---

### Level 2: Product Discovery
*Authority to determine features and user experience*

Signs:
- Conducts user research and product discovery
- Uses outcome-based roadmaps, personas, opportunity solution trees
- Can decline feature requests that don't meet strategic objectives
- Works toward a strategy set by someone else (head of product)

Verdict: **Minimum level required** for product managers and product owners to genuinely manage the product.

---

### Level 3: Product Strategy
*Authority to determine the product strategy itself*

Signs:
- Does market research, competitive analysis, business modelling
- Owns the product vision and strategy
- Uses KPIs to track if the strategy is working
- Operates within business and portfolio strategy constraints

Verdict: **Full-stack empowerment.** Generally recommended — allows maximum value creation and fast innovation.

---

## How to Level Up

Two levers:
1. **Expert power** — deep product management skills (strategy, tactics, leadership)
2. **Referent power** — trust and respect from peers, stakeholders, management

Both are required. Organisational change (role redefinition, leadership backing) is often also necessary to move from Level 1 → 2.

---

## Why It Matters

- Level-one PMs produce outputs but cannot influence value
- Level-two PMs can manage the product but execute a strategy set above them
- Level-three PMs own the entire value chain — strategy informs discovery, discovery informs delivery, execution informs strategy

Head-of-Product as sole strategy owner creates a bottleneck. Better: coach contributors to make strategic decisions themselves.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Level 2 is the minimum** — without discovery authority, a PM cannot meaningfully manage value
- **Level 3 is the goal** — full-stack empowerment enables fast innovation and closes the strategy-execution gap
- Expert + referent power are both needed to level up; organisational change is often required too
- Head of Product should develop contributors' strategy skills, not hoard strategy decisions
