# Product Strategy and Product Discovery

**Source:** Roman Pichler — [Product Strategy and Product Discovery](https://www.romanpichler.com/blog/product-strategy-and-product-discovery/) (2023)

---

## Summary

Product discovery without a validated strategy produces the wrong opportunities. Strategy provides the business outcome (the root node of discovery work), filters which opportunities matter, and front-loads much of the user research discovery requires. The relationship is bidirectional: discovery insights also inform strategy evolution.

---

## What is Product Discovery?

"Figuring out a solution to a problem we've been asked to solve" (Cagan).

Teresa Torres' **Opportunity Solution Tree (OST)** is the canonical tool:
- **Outcome** — the business goal to achieve (the root)
- **Opportunities** — customer needs that, if addressed, achieve the outcome
- **Solutions** — product capabilities that solve the needs

Problem: if the outcome (business goal) is wrong, every opportunity and solution derived from it is also wrong.

---

## What is a Validated Product Strategy?

A strategy that answers four questions:
- Who is the product for?
- Why would they want to use it?
- How does it benefit the business?
- What makes it stand out from alternatives?

**Validation** = iteratively testing the strategy against the biggest risks (target segment too small, need not compelling enough, standout features too weak) until empirical evidence backs the key assumptions.

Tool: the **Product Vision Board** — captures target group, needs, product, and business goals in one view.

---

## How Strategy Enables Discovery

1. **The right outcome** — validated business goals from the strategy become the root node of the OST. This removes the most dangerous failure mode: running good discovery on the wrong problem.

2. **Pre-validated opportunities** — strategy validation requires direct user research. This front-loads discovery: by the time you start the OST, you already know the key user needs.

3. **Opportunity filters** — every OST opportunity should be a step toward the needs captured in the strategy. The strategy provides the filter.

4. **Sub-goal derivation** — if a business goal is too large for one OST, derive a sub-goal. The strategy gives you permission to do so coherently.

---

## Bidirectional Feedback

Discovery informs strategy too:
- If the team cannot achieve the outcome or the chosen opportunities, the strategy may be invalid
- Sustained discovery failure is a signal to revisit the strategy, not just iterate on solutions

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Strategy precedes discovery** — validating the business outcome before starting the OST prevents discovering the right solution to the wrong problem
- **Validation is not a phase** — it's an iterative risk-reduction process that runs in parallel with discovery
- The Product Vision Board and the Opportunity Solution Tree are complementary: the Board feeds the OST
- Discovery and strategy are **continuously connected** — insights from discovery should feed back into strategy evolution
- Without a strategy, product discovery produces technically correct but strategically irrelevant outputs
