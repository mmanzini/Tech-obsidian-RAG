# Product Manager as Builder

**Source:** Roman Pichler — [Should Product Managers be Product Builders?](https://www.romanpichler.com/blog/product-managers-product-builders/) (2026)

---

## Summary

A *product builder* is a PM who actively co-creates the product — making prototypes, generating code, building mock-ups — rather than solely directing others. AI tools make this more accessible than ever. But building software should not displace a PM's core job: understanding the problem and maximising the value the product creates.

---

## What is a Product Builder?

- An emerging role (Amazon, LinkedIn among early adopters)
- Uses AI tools (Lovable, Claude Code, Figma) to prototype, vibe-code, and validate ideas directly
- Requires skills traditionally owned by designers and engineers
- Distinct from Agile product ownership and continuous discovery — the co-creation element is new

---

## The Case For

| Benefit | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| **Higher productivity** | Smaller teams; PM can do more without waiting |
| **Shorter time to market** | Eliminates handoff delays; prototype replaces user story as validation artifact |
| **Better products** | Less strategy-execution chasm; closer collaboration with engineers; intent preserved through delivery |

---

## The Case Against

| Risk | What goes wrong |
|---|---|
| **Strategy neglect** | Building absorbs attention; the question "should we build this?" gets displaced by "how fast can I build this?" |
| **Technical debt** | Prototypes vibe-coded without architecture awareness; AI-generated code that is hard to maintain |
| **Role confusion and burnout** | PM steps into design/engineering domains, undermining team autonomy; already-stretched role gets heavier |

---

## The Right Balance

Building software should not be the main focus.

**The PM's real job:** maximise the value the product creates — by empathising with users, synthesising feedback, deciding which problems matter, and setting clear product outcomes.

AI tools are most useful for:
- **Strategy validation** — quick prototypes to test assumptions during discovery
- **Accelerating product discovery** — compress the gap between idea and user feedback

The builder approach works best on:
- Early lifecycle stage products
- Comparatively small, loosely coupled products
- Environments where the PM can acquire builder skills without abandoning strategy

---

## The Prerequisite: Strategy First

Without a clear, validated product strategy, building fast leads to building the wrong thing fast.

> "Strategy guides discovery and delivery. The three areas of product management must be balanced."

Building capability amplified by AI is dangerous without strategy discipline. AI allows you to create a full app in an hour — the question is whether you should, and what problem it's solving.

---

## Key Takeaways

- The product builder role is real and offers genuine benefits — especially for validating ideas faster
- **Strategy neglect is the primary risk** — building focus crowds out the work that determines what to build
- AI tools are best used to accelerate discovery (prototypes, experiments) not to replace it
- Head of Product responsibility: remove document overhead so PMs can do real product work, not outsource it to builder tasks
- Balance is contextual — portfolios differ; some products are better candidates for the builder approach than others
