# Build Your PM Second Brain with ChatGPT

**Source:** Amir Klein (PM, monday.com), guest post on [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/how-to-build-your-pm-second-brain-with-chatgpt) (2025-12-16)

How a monday.com PM used ChatGPT Projects to offload the "giant basket of context" PMs carry — and free working memory for judgement and taste.

## The problem: context is the job, but carrying it is the tax

PMs assemble fragments (user feedback, metrics, past decisions, intuition, Slack threads, decks) into clear problem statements. Doing that synthesis *in your head* caps thinking at working-memory size. **When context lives outside your head but is retrievable, you don't remember more — you reason better.**

Klein's monday.com brief: build the company's first AI co-pilot. Context was scattered across Slack, Notion, decks, dashboards. Dumping everything into a ChatGPT Project was the unlock — it surfaced the key user pain point (customers lacked confidence, not capability) within minutes.

## The three-step setup

### 1. Create its personality (system instructions)
Meta-trick: ask ChatGPT to *write* the instructions for you. Describe what you want (role, tone, pushback style, domain expertise), paste the output into the Project's instructions.

Example Klein used: "*sharp, smart, fun, not always agreeing... pro at product management — product sense, execution, taste, delight.*"

### 2. Feed it information
**Biggest reframe: everything is text.** PRDs obviously, but also:
- Decks → export as PDF
- Websites / product docs → Cmd+P → save as PDF
- Slack channels → export or copy-paste into a doc → PDF
- Research transcripts, surveys, CSVs, Reddit threads
- **Feed artifacts you *generated* back in** — the brain becomes a living thing that grows as you work

### 3. Let it cook
Three recurring workflows Klein uses:
- **Sign-up forms / surveys.** Project knows the initiative; it drafts 2–3 concise questions that gauge expectation alignment.
- **Prototypes.** "Write me a [[ai-product-development/_index|Lovable]] prompt for this." Drop in Figma screenshots. Copy the output into Lovable. Working prototype in 10 minutes.
- **Tailored comms.** One initiative, five audiences (marketing blurb, QBR 3-sentence, sales enablement deck, user-research script, leadership update). The "mental reload tax" disappears — word-dump once, generate all five.

## The mental model — context is the new interface

- The second brain is an **instantaneous librarian** (retrieves the right piece) + an **associate PM** (runs analysis, generates recommendations).
- Does **not** outsource judgement or creativity — still you making every decision.
- Removes friction between *what you know* and *how you need to express it*.
- Tasks that ate half a day now take minutes.

## Works in any tool

- **ChatGPT** → Projects (left sidebar → New project)
- **Claude** → Projects (left sidebar → New project, name + description + instructions)
- **Gemini** → Gems (left panel)

Principles are tool-agnostic: instructions, files, iterate.

## Key Takeaways

- **Offload context, keep judgement.** The second brain holds information and reasoning scaffolding; you still decide.
- **Everything is text.** The reframe that unlocks how much context you can feed in — decks, Slack, docs, websites all become PDFs.
- **Meta-prompt the instructions.** Ask the model to write its own system prompt for your role; tweak and paste.
- **Feed outputs back in.** PRDs and docs you generate should go back into the Project — it compounds into a living knowledge base.
- **Best leverage: tailored communications and rapid prototypes** — both are "context reload" tasks that AI nukes.
- AI "extends" the PM; does not replace product sense, taste, or customer intuition.
