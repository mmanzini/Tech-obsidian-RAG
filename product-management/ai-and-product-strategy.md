# AI and Product Strategy

**Source:** Roman Pichler — [How to Use AI to Create a Winning Product Strategy](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/how-use-ai-create-winning-product-strategy-roman-pichler-3qfme/) (2024)

---

## Summary

AI accelerates strategy work but does not replace strategic thinking. Product teams that skip learning what an effective strategy looks like — and just bolt on AI tools — will produce faster outputs toward the wrong goal. The pre-requisite is understanding the strategy workflow; only then can AI be integrated purposefully.

---

## Why Strategy Matters More in the AI Era

Four reasons strategy is now more important, not less:

- **Guardrails for AI use** — strategy determines which AI experiments to run and which features to test; without it, teams throw things at the wall
- **Stakeholder alignment** — developers, designers, and stakeholders all have different ideas; strategy keeps them in the same direction
- **Avoids tech for tech's sake** — a good strategy asks "does AI truly benefit the users?" before building
- **Ethical guidance** — bias, privacy, environmental impact must be addressed at the strategy level

---

## The Strategy Workflow

Two elements (not phases):

| Element | Purpose | When |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategy Discovery** | Create or significantly change a strategy (new product, new market, PMF push) | High innovation, high uncertainty |
| **Continuous Strategizing** | Systematically evolve an existing strategy in response to market signals | Ongoing, weekly |

Both require learning and adaptation. Strategy Discovery adds experimentation. Continuous Strategizing relies more on product analytics and monitoring.

---

## Where AI Can Help

| Area | AI role | Sample tools |
|---|---|---|
| Market & competitive intelligence | Automate tracking and analysis of competitor data | AlphaSense, Crayon AI, Klue |
| Customer insight mining | Extract actionable themes from interviews, tickets, reviews | Claude, Dovetail, Insight7 |
| Trend analysis | Identify emerging patterns in market and behaviour | Brandwatch, Google Trends |
| Product analytics | Track user interactions; check if strategy is working | Amplitude, Heap, Mixpanel |
| Vibe coding | Test ideas quickly via prototype generation | Cursor, Figma, Loveable |

AI applies to both Strategy Discovery and Continuous Strategizing — but with different tools. Vibe coding and experimentation suit Discovery; analytics suit Continuous Strategizing.

---

## What AI Cannot Replace

Four hard limits:

1. **Empathy and intuition** — direct user observation and interviews remain irreplaceable for understanding real needs
2. **Data dependency** — disruptive innovations create new markets without historical data; AI has nothing to extrapolate from
3. **Accuracy** — AI confidently fabricates market data, customer quotes, and case studies; outputs must be verified before use in strategic decisions
4. **Ethics and privacy** — feeding sensitive data into external AI tools risks IP leaks and compliance violations; AI-generated strategies may encode training biases

---

## Key Takeaways

- **AI is a strategy accelerator, not a strategist** — it cannot set the right business goal or empathise with users
- The precondition for good AI-assisted strategy is understanding what a good strategy looks like — "you have to know what a good running technique looks like to take full advantage of new running shoes"
- Don't skip direct user and customer interactions to save time; don't skip review of AI-generated insights before using them strategically
- **Build AI into the workflow, don't bolt on tools** — purposeful integration outperforms ad-hoc tool use
- AI-generated personas are explicitly not recommended — persona quality depends on first-hand research, not synthesis
