# Product Manager Is an Unfair Role — So Work Unfairly

**Source:** Tal Raviv (early PM at Patreon, Riverside, Wix, AppsFlyer), guest post on [Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/product-manager-is-an-unfair-role-so-work-unfairly) (2024-11-12)

Seven tactics from a career "super-IC" PM for thriving in the flatter, AI-accelerated PM role — where one IC is expected to own larger surface area with no more hours.

## The frame

PMs juggle maker + manager schedules, can't hire/fire but must ship, and absorb org dysfunction. The "great flattening" (fewer ICs owning more, AI compressing expectations further) makes fairness worse, not better. Survival = building your own systems to make the job work for you.

## The seven tactics

### 1. Do the action items **live in the meeting**
Screen-share your Slack/Jira/calendar. Draft the summary, the ticket, the follow-up ping, or the next meeting's scheduler *while everyone watches*. Hit enter before the call ends.

- Stakeholders appreciate it — you look decisive and aligned.
- Eliminates the post-meeting to-do tail that eats evenings.

### 2. 59-second Looms replace half your meetings
When a topic is nuanced enough to need voice but doesn't need synchronous debate:
- Aim <60 seconds; film multiple takes
- In the Slack message, pitch the brevity and time-ROI to the viewer
- Treat your audience as an audience — intentional about view + click-through

### 3. Hide, ignore, automate Slack
- **No Slack in the first half of your day.** Ironclad rule across 3 hypergrowth companies.
- **Sections + collapse** → look at the critical channels first, scan the rest calmly.
- **Hide read channels** — the most underused Slack setting.
- **Slack reminders = robot EA.** Friendly-bump ghosted threads so you never drop the ball and build an "I got this" aura.

### 4. Cultivate a team that operates without you
- **"Incredible products have been built without a PM."** Tell the team. Mean it.
- When someone asks for a decision, flip it: they are the expert; what context do they need from *you*?
- When a non-PM does something technically "your job" (data analyst proposes A/B tests, designer talks to stakeholder, QA does analysis) — **shower them with positivity**.
- **DMs are the devil.** Ask senders to repost in public channels. Transparency, collaboration, discoverability, and culture-shaping in one move.

### 5. Product scrapbooking — discovery in advance
Keep a Notion database of feedback screenshots, charts, support tickets, Gong links, Slack threads — organised by swim lane (monetization, onboarding, virality, etc.). Scraps are lightweight, titled for future-you search.

Payoffs:
- Instant real-world context when someone suggests an idea
- Kickoff decks write themselves when the initiative gets prioritised
- Quarterly planning: filter by swim lane → strategic context from raw signal in minutes
- Side effect: stakeholders notice you listen → send more signal

### 6. Let AI write for you, but don't let it read for you
- **Write PRDs, Jira tickets, user stories, blog posts** via ChatGPT + dictation (Whisper, BetterDictation, Superwhisper). PMs have tons of context and talk all day — speech-to-text matches how you already work.
- Three ready-made prompts in the post: PRD template, user story template, blog-post-from-notes (preserve your voice, no AI-speak).
- **DON'T outsource the reading.** Summarising raw Slack / support / user-research transcripts removes your competitive advantage — customer absorption and product intuition. Stay in the weeds on inputs.

### 7. PM your own brain's freshness
- Rest hard with the same discipline you bring to work.
- Uninstall Slack mobile (a Patreon Erica Baker move — she led Slack's infrastructure team without it).
- **Display name as `Tal Raviv OOO until Nov 15`** — more effective than a status, because people see it as they @-you.
- Physical transitions (walk, stretch, instrument) — same-chair "rest" isn't rest.
- Full weekend / week-long PTO unlocks creativity the Monday after.

Gloria Mark ("Attention Span"): "*When you can't psychologically detach from work, it's a lot harder to psychologically reattach the next morning.*"

## Key Takeaways

- **Work unfairly** = build private systems that make the role livable, because fairness isn't coming.
- **Do work inside meetings, not after them.** The single highest-leverage tactic.
- **Public channels over DMs**, always. Productivity + culture shaping + team autonomy.
- **AI writes, you read.** Preserve the customer-contact muscle; outsource the scribe work.
- **Super-IC** PMs who master these systems thrive in the flat, AI-native org.
- Protect rest like you protect focus — freshness compounds.
