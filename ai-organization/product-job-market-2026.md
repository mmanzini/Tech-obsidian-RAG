# State of the Product Job Market 2026

Lenny Rachitsky's biannual report (March 2026) on tech hiring trends, based on TrueUp data covering 9,000+ tech companies. Surprisingly the most optimistic edition to date.

## Headline Trends

1. **PM openings at 3-year highs.** 7,300+ open PM roles globally — 75% above the early-2023 low and up ~20% since the start of 2026. Most open PM roles since 2022.
2. **AI hasn't slowed eng demand (yet).** 67,000+ open eng roles globally; 26,000 in the US. Growth is *accelerating*.
3. **Recruiter demand is back.** Tech recruiter openings approaching 2022 peaks — leading indicator that sustained hiring growth is likely.
4. **AI roles are hockey-sticking.** Both AI-company roles and AI-specific roles inside non-AI companies are exploding. AI engineer and AI PM demand similarly vertical.
5. **Design has plateaued.** ~5,700 open design roles globally; flat since early 2023. PM-to-designer ratio flipped in mid-2023 and PM demand keeps pulling away (1.27x). Likely AI-related — fast-moving eng leaves less room for traditional design process.
6. **Bay Area share is *growing*.** 20%+ of eng/design and 23%+ of PM roles are now Bay Area (PM share up 50% since 2022). A third of AI roles are Bay Area.
7. **NYC #2 globally**, despite headquartering none of the leading companies. International hubs: Bengaluru, London, Tel Aviv, Singapore.
8. **Remote work continues to decline.**
9. **Overall tech jobs are growing** despite ongoing layoff headlines.

## Key Takeaways

- The "AI is killing tech jobs" narrative is not supported by openings data — at least in early 2026.
- PM and eng demand are both rising, but the [[anthropic-growth-takeaways|PM/eng ratio is compressing]] — more openings may not mean equal leverage.
- Design plateau is the most striking divergence; worth watching as a signal of how AI reshapes org composition.
- Geographic concentration is *increasing* even as remote tooling improves — the Bay Area's gravity for AI is structural, not cyclical.
