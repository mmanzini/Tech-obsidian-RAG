# Nikhyl Singhal — Smiling exhaustion and the PM renaissance

Source: [[lennys-podcast]] interview with Nikhyl Singhal (ex-VP Product at Meta, Google, founder of The Skip).

Singhal's read on the PM job market: a bifurcation is underway. Builders are having the best year of their careers; career-PMs are heading for an existential crisis. The next 12–24 months will be chaotic, then stabilise.

## Smiling exhaustion
- PMs spent years exhausted by alignment meetings, influence-without-authority, org overhead
- Now the work is genuinely fun again — people build, compensation is up
- But the pace is relentless and the ground shifts every quarter
- The good PMs are exhausted and smiling; that's the new baseline

## 12–24 months of chaos, then stabilisation
- Comparable to the 2000s internet-era reinvention of product management
- Every three months: new agents, new forms of judgment, blurred role boundaries
- In ~2 years, companies will have optimised around the new operating model
- You don't need to predict the end-state, just cross the transition

## The bifurcation
- Two kinds of PMs: those who joined because they love building, those who joined because it pays well
- Roughly half the industry is in the second bucket — facing existential risk
- If sitting down and making something great doesn't energise you, the industry is moving away from you
- See [[product-management]] and [[ai-organization]]

## Tens of thousands out, thousands back in — all AI-first
- Prediction: large companies lay off far more than they rehire (e.g., 30K out, 8K back)
- The rehires are AI-native builders across every function
- Resume-logo capital from the 2010s is depreciating; "how modern are you?" matters more than where you worked

## The interview question has changed
- Old: "Five years ago you shipped X — walk me through it"
- New: "Put yourself in this scenario right now — what tools do you use, how do you move?"
- Six years at a prestigious company using outdated ways of working is now a liability, not an asset

## PMs as dandelion seeds across industries
- Marketing, sales, HR, PE-owned HVAC rollups, schools — everyone realises they need to change how they build
- Who leads the transformation? People with builder judgment — often PMs
- Counter-flow: designers, data scientists, and engineers move into product roles
- Titles will collapse; function lines blur
- See [[ai-organization]] and [[way-of-working]]

## Compensation and opportunity at an all-time high (for the right PMs)
- Strongest builders have more offers than ever
- Paths opening to founder, CEO, or cross-functional executive roles
- Other functions now want PM backgrounds because they need builder judgment embedded

## Key Takeaways
- The PM job is in a renaissance AND a crisis simultaneously — depends which half you're in
- Next 12–24 months are chaotic; stabilisation follows around 2027
- Layoffs will be massive; rehires will be a fraction and entirely AI-native
- "How modern are you?" is replacing resume logos as the hiring signal
- PMs will diffuse into every industry as agents of transformation
