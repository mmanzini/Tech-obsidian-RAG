# Garrett Lord — Audience is the only moat

Source: [[lennys-podcast]] interview with Garrett Lord (co-founder & CEO Handshake).

Handshake AI went from $0 to $50M ARR in four months, sitting inside a $200M core careers business. Lord's thesis: when models commoditise, the only durable moat is privileged access to an audience — in his case, students and early-career experts.

## The only moat is audience
- Models, tools, and frameworks are commoditising on a 3-month cycle
- The durable assets are the audiences a founder already has warm access to
- Handshake's existing relationship with 500K PhDs and ~3M Master's students became the wedge into the expert-data market
- Lesson: before building a new AI product, audit what audiences you already uniquely reach

## Handshake AI's lab priorities: Quality, Volume, Speed
- Frontier labs buy on three dimensions in strict priority order: Quality > Volume > Speed
- Quality = credentialed expert, rubric-validated output
- Volume = ability to staff 1000s of annotators on short notice
- Speed = turnaround in days not weeks
- Most competitors optimise for the wrong dimension first
- See [[ai-product-development]] and [[agent-workflows]]

## Trajectories and rubrics over raw labels
- Labs no longer buy "label this image"; they buy full agent trajectories (multi-step task completions) scored against rubrics
- A good rubric decomposes a task into 20–50 checkable sub-conditions
- Rubric authoring is its own senior craft — often more valuable than the labels themselves

## "Separate everything"
- Lord keeps the AI business operationally separate from the core careers business
- Different hiring, different compensation bands, different KPIs
- New S-curve businesses die when forced to inherit the parent's operating rhythm
- See [[ai-organization]]

## "Leave nothing to chance" + the Princeton pool story
- Lord's operating posture: rehearse, pre-wire, remove ambiguity before any high-stakes moment
- Princeton pool story: he walked every room of a target customer's office before a pitch to remove surprise variables
- Pairs well with high-agency culture — the opposite of vibes-based execution

## Key Takeaways
- In an AI market, audience is the only durable moat — models and tools commoditise
- Labs buy on Quality > Volume > Speed, in that order — optimise accordingly
- Agent trajectories scored against rubrics are replacing raw label sales
- New S-curve businesses must be operationally separated from the parent
- "Leave nothing to chance" — pre-wire high-stakes moments, don't improvise
