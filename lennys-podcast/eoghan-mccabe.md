# Eoghan McCabe — The sharp knife and the 99¢ agent

Source: [[lennys-podcast]] interview with Eoghan McCabe (founder & CEO Intercom).

McCabe reshaped Intercom around Fin, an AI support agent that hit $100M ARR in under three quarters — built six weeks after GPT-3.5 shipped. The story is half product (outcome pricing) and half ego-death leadership rewrite.

## Fin: zero to $100M in under three quarters
- Prototype started six weeks after GPT-3.5 launched
- The wedge: replace human agents on tier-1 tickets, not augment them
- Priced at 99¢ per successful resolution — originally losing ~120¢ per call on inference; bet the model costs would fall, and they did
- Outcome pricing aligned the buyer (CFO) with the product's actual value proposition
- See [[ai-product-development]] on outcome-based pricing

## The sharp knife rewrite
- McCabe returned as CEO and rewrote Intercom's values around "sharp knife" — fast, precise, sometimes cuts
- ~40% staff turnover during the transition; he frames it as necessary not regrettable
- Lesson: when the product thesis changes (SaaS support → autonomous agent), the culture must also change or it will drag
- See [[ai-organization]] and [[way-of-working]]

## Ego death as a leadership technology
- 12 years of therapy; explicit practice of ego dissolution
- Argues most founder pathologies (avoiding hard calls, hiring mirrors, confusing identity with company) are ego problems, not strategy problems
- "Quitter" origin: McCabe quit previous companies repeatedly before Intercom — reframes quitting as a signal-quality mechanism

## Intercom as founder factory
- Invented/popularised RICE prioritisation and Jobs-to-be-Done adoption in product ops
- Dozens of Intercom alumni now run their own companies — McCabe attributes it to explicit operator apprenticeship
- See [[product-management]] on RICE and JTBD

## Betting on falling model costs
- The 99¢ price was set knowing it lost money that quarter — a bet on the cost curve, not current margins
- Generalisable rule: in an AI product, price for the margin you expect in 12 months, not the one you have today
- If your unit economics require today's model prices, you're underwriting the wrong curve

## Key Takeaways
- Fin hit $100M ARR in <3 quarters; prototype in 6 weeks off GPT-3.5
- Outcome pricing (99¢/resolution) aligns buyer, product, and model-cost trajectory
- When product thesis shifts to agents, the values need a sharp-knife rewrite — expect high turnover
- Ego death is an under-discussed founder capability; McCabe treats it as infrastructure
- Price for the margin you expect in 12 months, not today's cost
