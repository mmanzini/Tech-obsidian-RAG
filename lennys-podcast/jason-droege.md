# Jason Droege — Data Labeling, Enterprise AI, and New Business Bets

**Source:** Lenny's Podcast — CEO of Scale AI (first interview post-Meta deal); founder of Uber Eats

Jason took over Scale AI after the $14B Meta deal that moved Alex Wang and ~15 people to Meta. Scale remains independent, 1,100 people, growing every month. Before Scale he launched Uber Eats (0 → $20B in 4.5 years) and co-founded Scour with Travis Kalanick. The episode blends an insider view of AI labs with hard-won lessons on starting new businesses.

## Expert Data Labeling Is the New Default

- **18 months ago:** label a short story, rank it vs. another.
- **Today:** a single task is an elite web dev building a full site, or a PhD explaining an oncology nuance.
- **80% of Scale's contributor network has a bachelor's+; 15% have PhDs.**
- "Competitors position that Scale still does generalist work — that's bogus."
- Data labeling has always been a **history of new beginnings** — each time models get better, the type of data they need changes.

## RL Environments as the Next Wave

- The field is moving from "models that **know** things" to "models that **do** things."
- **RL environments** = sandboxes where agents act toward a goal (navigate a Salesforce instance, book a meeting, resolve a claim).
- Scale's year-long research: how **generalizable** is a given task? Find the workflows that transfer — don't label 45 trillion combinations.
- Inside enterprises: companies now need their own labelers to **digitize internal judgment** (same document means different things at bank A vs. bank B).

## The 60-70-80 Trap in Enterprise AI

- POCs get to 60–70% accuracy; humans assume the last 30% is trivial. It isn't.
- Every additional "nine" of accuracy is an order-of-magnitude investment.
- Realistic enterprise AI timelines: **6–12 months** to production readiness.
- MIT's 95%-of-AI-projects-fail stat is partly clickbait — denominator is bloated by cheap failed pilots.

## Starting a Business: Insight Before Everything

- Filtering heuristic: *"Why am I so lucky to have this insight that a million other entrepreneurs don't?"*
- Second filter: *"Why do I want to work on this for 5–10 years?"*
- Keep a **wide aperture** on ideas as long as possible — don't commit early.
- At Uber he piloted delivery vans of convenience goods in DC (failed — no cigarettes/beer/Slurpees); generic point-to-point delivery (failed). Food delivery clicked.

## The Uber Eats Ingredient Teardown

- Restaurants wouldn't share unit economics. So the team **ordered food, weighed every ingredient**, matched against supplier catalogs.
- Discovered: ~20-30% ingredients, ~20-30% labor, ~10% rent — and **70-80% incremental gross margin** on incremental demand.
- That math gave them the confidence to charge restaurants 30% (everyone screamed; clearing price turned out to be ~25%).
- Lesson: customers won't volunteer their economics; **triangulate independently**.

## The Gross Margin Filter

- Coarse but powerful: *"Start at 60% gross margin. Why doesn't that work?"*
- If answer is "the customer has an alternative at 20%," your margin will compress — act accordingly.
- High gross margin + healthy churn curve = strongest signal of defensible value-add.

## "Not Losing Is a Precursor to Winning"

- Tech culture rewards "just go for it" — investor-led narrative.
- Best operators make **asymmetrically positive** bets; they think about downside before upside.
- Survival > heroics. Most companies die before their timing/insight lands.
- Customers will still exist in 5 years. You need to still exist to serve them.

## McDonald's Stubbornness Story

- McDonald's approached Uber Eats; Jason said no for 4–5 months (vision was indie restaurants).
- The delay accidentally produced **exclusive terms**.
- Business hockey-sticked a second time after the launch.
- Lesson: ignorance and conviction sometimes produce better deal terms than expertise would.

## Key Takeaways

- Data labeling has moved decisively to PhD/expert work; generalist labeling is last-decade framing.
- Enterprise AI POCs succeed at 60-70% and fail at 95% — every extra "nine" is 10x effort and months.
- New-business filter: unique insight + 5-10 year willingness + 60%+ gross margin.
- Triangulate customer economics independently — they'll never hand you the real numbers.
- Survival is the precursor to winning; avoid asymmetrically negative bets.
- The world is moving from models that know things to models that do things — RL environments are where the next alpha lives.
