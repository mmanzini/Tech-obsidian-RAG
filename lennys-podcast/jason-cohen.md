# Jason Cohen — A Six-Question Diagnostic for Stalled SaaS Growth

**Source:** Lenny's Podcast — Founder WP Engine, Smart Bear; serial SaaS operator

Jason's frame: stalled growth is almost never where the founder thinks it is. He runs a sequenced six-question diagnostic — and most companies find the answer in questions 1-3, long before they start blaming marketing or pricing.

## The Six-Question Diagnostic (In Order)

1. **Is logo churn capping the business?**
2. **Is it pricing or positioning?**
3. **Is NRR / existing-customer growth broken?**
4. **Are marketing channels saturated?**
5. **Is the target market wrong?**
6. **Do you actually need to grow?**

Always work top-down. Skipping to #4 while #1 is broken is how companies burn years.

## Logo-Churn Cap Math

- Hard ceiling: **max customers = new customers per period ÷ cancel rate per period**.
- At 5% monthly churn and 100 new/month you cap at 2,000 customers — forever.
- Most stalled companies have a churn problem masquerading as a growth problem.
- Implication: fixing churn is usually higher leverage than adding channels.

## "What Made You Cancel" vs "Why Did You Cancel"

- Groove SaaS: moved cancel-survey response from **10% to 20%** by switching the verb.
- "Why" triggers justification mode — you get rationalized, polite answers.
- "What made you" triggers narrative mode — you get the actual event sequence.
- Never accept "too expensive" at face value — price is almost never the real reason.

## Price Is Almost Never the Real Reason

- When users say "too expensive" they mean "I didn't see enough value."
- Real drivers: onboarding friction, missing feature they expected, a bad support moment.
- Implication: the lever is positioning/activation, not discounting.
- Discounting a positioning problem makes the unit economics worse without fixing churn.

## Onboarding Has Outsized Churn Leverage

- First 7-30 days determine the long-run retention curve.
- Small activation improvements compound into massive LTV gains.
- Often the single highest-ROI fix when question #1 (logo churn) is flagged.
- Monitor time-to-first-value as the activation north star.

## The Double Down Rebrand Story — Positioning Over Pricing

- Same product: half the AdWords spend at $5K price = doubled leads at $40K price.
- Same ad dollars generated **8x price for identical product** after repositioning.
- Lesson: positioning sets the frame that makes any price feel reasonable or outrageous.
- If you have a "pricing problem," you almost certainly have a positioning problem.

## NRR Asymmetry and the 100% Line

- **Median SaaS IPO NRR: 119%** — public markets expect expansion revenue.
- NRR ≥100% is non-negotiable at scale; anything less means you're running up a down escalator.
- Loss asymmetry: a 20% revenue loss requires a **25% gain** to recover — asymmetry compounds.
- Lever order: reduce gross churn → add expansion motion → then scale acquisition.

## Key Takeaways

- Run the six questions in order — skipping ahead wastes years.
- Logo churn × new customers = hard company cap; do the math first.
- Ask "what made you cancel" — doubles response rate and reveals causes.
- "Too expensive" is rarely the real reason; positioning usually is.
- Onboarding is the highest-leverage churn fix.
- Repositioning can 8x price for an identical product (Double Down).
- NRR ≥100% is the table-stakes bar; median IPO SaaS is at 119%.
