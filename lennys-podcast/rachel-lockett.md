# Rachel Lockett — Coach, don't advise

Source: [[lennys-podcast]] interview with Rachel Lockett (executive coach, ex-Stripe top-talent program).

Lockett's core argument: most tech leaders default to advising when they should be coaching, and optimise careers around ladders when they should be optimising around gifts. She hands over several concrete frameworks (GROW, NVC, 80% in gifts, vows) ready to use.

## Coach vs advise
- Advising is faster but creates low buy-in; coaching produces commitment because the person owns the conclusion
- Great managers ask explicitly: "Do you want advice, space to think, or my recommendation?"
- Unsolicited advice is almost always counterproductive

## Three levels of listening
- Level 1 — internal: listening to your own reactions while the other person speaks
- Level 2 — focused: fully on the speaker, what they say and don't say
- Level 3 — global: also tracking energy, body, room, what's unsaid
- Leaders default to Level 1; Level 2–3 are trainable

## GROW model
- Goal — what outcome does the person want from this conversation
- Reality — what's actually happening now
- Options — what paths exist (generated by them, not you)
- Way forward — specific next step with a commitment
- Works in a 20-minute 1:1, not just formal coaching

## 80% in your gifts
- Target: spend 80% of working time in activities that energise you and play to natural strengths
- Two-week exercise: each night list 5 things that gave energy, 5 that drained it — patterns emerge
- Supplement with "when I walk in the room, what shows up?" email to 5–10 people who know you well
- Lockett's own pivot: offered a PM role at Remind, realised her gift was people not product strategy, became a coach
- See [[way-of-working]]

## Co-founder vows and operating rhythm
- 65% of startups fail from co-founder conflict; the relationship needs explicit maintenance
- Recommend explicit "vows" — how we'll make decisions, handle conflict, show up
- Weekly check-ins, quarterly in-person retrospectives; "get off the dance floor onto the balcony"
- One-page plan: vision/values | strategy/KPIs | annual goals | quarterly goals — borrowed from Alpine Investors' People First Operating Rhythm

## Nonviolent Communication for hard conversations
- Four steps: Observation → Feeling → Need → Request
- Observation = a fact a camera could capture, no interpretation
- Feeling = an actual emotion (not "I feel like you're a jerk" — that's a judgment)
- Goal of any conflict is mutual understanding, not winning the argument
- Stripe's filter: "Would I enthusiastically rehire this person for this role?" — a binary gut-check that clarifies talent decisions

## Key Takeaways
- Coach don't advise; always ask which mode the person wants
- Aim for 80% of your time in your gifts — use a two-week energy log to find them
- Co-founder relationships need vows and a weekly/quarterly rhythm, not just shared equity
- NVC's Observation → Feeling → Need → Request is the cleanest hard-conversation template
- "Would I enthusiastically rehire this person?" is the binary talent filter
