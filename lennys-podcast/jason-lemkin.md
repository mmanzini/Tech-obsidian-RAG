# Jason Lemkin — The SaaStr Sales Hiring Playbook

**Source:** Lenny's Podcast — Founder, SaaStr; ex-CEO EchoSign

Jason has seen the same hiring mistakes in SaaS sales for 15 years. His rules are blunt, opinionated, and evergreen — optimized for founders who think sales is "icky" and therefore keep delegating it wrong.

## Founders Are A+ Middlers

- Most founders are bad at cold open and bad at close, but **A+ at the middle** of a sales call.
- Customers love talking to the CEO — you don't get to meet Benioff, but you get to meet the founder.
- You can run circles around 98% of Salesforce/HubSpot reps on product depth. Use it.
- Milestone before hiring: **close the first 10 customers yourself** or approach 100% chance of failure on VP Sales.

## Hire Two, Not One — and the Rule of 30

- Always hire **two reps at once** — you can't A/B test humans with a sample size of 1.
- Expect to interview ~30 candidates: 20 are broken, 8 are mediocre, 1–2 are magicians.
- The only test that matters: **"Would I buy my own product from this person?"** — ignore LinkedIn pedigree.
- Best early rep is often a quirky pyromaniac living in their brother's garage, not the Twilio alum.

## Last Product Harder Than Yours

- **Single most important hiring hint**: hire someone whose previous product was *harder* to sell than yours.
- Example: Sam Blond sold cloud financial software (Intacct) before joining EchoSign — signatures felt effortless by comparison.
- A rep selling down from harder-to-easier is "weight removed from their feet." Selling up is catastrophe.

## Wait on the VP of Sales

- You need **two reps hitting quota** before hiring a VP Sales, or it's a Hail Mary.
- VP Sales job is scaling from 3 to 300, not finding PMF.
- VP Sales must carry a bag or you're in trouble — in 2024 too many veterans "don't want to sell anymore."
- First 30-day screening question: *"What do you want to do in your first two weeks?"* If they say "process/Salesforce/territory planning" and not "meet customers" — out.

## The Comp Rule: Close More Than You Take Home

- Obsessing over base vs. OTE is the rookie mistake — what matters is **close > take-home**.
- Target multiples: SMB 3x, mid-market 4x, enterprise 5x OTE.
- First-quarter ramp: let them keep **100% of what they close** for one quarter only. More than that and mediocre reps lean in.
- Don't be sad when your rep out-earns the founders — it means the math works.

## Sell Me This App

- Every candidate must do a real product demo before the final offer — no explainer-video pre-work = no offer.
- Reference checks have collapsed since 2020 — reinstate them.
- Jason tries to be the **last interviewer**, offer-in-hand, to make sure the candidate won't bail in 3 months.

## We're Not Selling Used Cars

- B2B sales is problem-solving, not persuasion. Best reps **tell prospects not to buy yet** if the product doesn't fit their moment.
- Selling a "Tesla Model 3 performance" = there's competition, it has gaps, you've answered 4 calls of questions, and you're confident it's the right choice — so you ask for the deal.

## Key Takeaways

- Close the first 10 customers yourself before hiring anyone.
- Hire two reps at a time; interview 30; the test is "would I buy from them?"
- Hire reps whose prior product was harder to sell than yours.
- Wait on VP Sales until two reps are hitting quota — otherwise ~0% success.
- First quarter: let new reps keep 100% of closed revenue. Never longer.
- Make every candidate demo the product before offer — no exceptions.
