# Howie Liu — The IC CEO and the fast-thinking / slow-thinking org

Source: `sources/howie-liu.md` (Lenny's Podcast, Airtable co-founder/CEO)

Liu's thesis: every pre-GenAI company must be **refounded**, and the CEO has to become an IC again to do it. His operating frameworks are the fast-thinking / slow-thinking reorg borrowed from Kahneman, the chief-tastemaker stance, a ruthless weekly AI-execution cadence, and the "if you founded this today" test for deciding whether to keep running the company at all.

## IC CEO — CEOs becoming individual contributors again
- Liu cut default 1:1s and replaced them with urgency-driven, topic-seeded meetings.
- Proudly the #1 inference-cost user of Airtable AI — intentionally wasteful because the value per dollar is extreme.
- Nick Turley's signal (per Dan Shipper): does the CEO use ChatGPT/Claude *daily*? Liu's answer: hourly.
- You can't taste the soup without helping cook it — demos and screenshots don't transmit what AI actually feels like.

## Fast-thinking / slow-thinking reorg
- Borrowed directly from Kahneman — Airtable's EPD split into two groups:
  - **Fast-thinking (AI Platform):** ship truly jaw-dropping new capabilities on a near-weekly cadence.
  - **Slow-thinking:** deliberate infra like HyperDB (multi-hundred-million-record data sets) that can't be hack-prototyped in a week.
- Both are necessary — fast creates top-of-funnel excitement, slow turns seeds into durable enterprise deployments.
- Replaced earlier business-unit reorg that still thought incrementally per surface area.

## "If you were founding this today" test
- The refounding question: if you started a new company with the same mission today, fully AI-native, how would you build it?
- If your existing assets help → refound inside the current company.
- If they don't → find a buyer and go start the next incarnation. Don't run a zombie.
- Airtable's answer: no-code primitives are a better substrate for AI business-app generation than one-shot code from scratch.

## Chief tastemaker
- CEO's job in an AI era isn't steering via OKRs — it's setting taste.
- Requires being in the details, not reading summaries. "You can't just look at screenshots of a pre-recorded video."
- Taste is transmitted by example — Liu shares his actual prompt chains, Replit landing pages, deep research outputs rather than polished memos.

## Play, not check-the-box
- Explicit permission: block a day or a week, cancel meetings, just play with every AI product you can find. Use Liu as the excuse.
- Play = curiosity mode, not completion mode. You learn more, and you find form factors that no roadmap would have given you.
- Try products beyond your domain (Runway, Sesame, HeyGen) — not because you'll ship voice mode, but to calibrate the landscape.

## Prototypes over decks
- Execution artifact shifted from PRD to functional prototype.
- A deck can claim "Omni will be great at X"; only a working prototype reveals whether it's too slow, needs a progress bar, or fails on realistic prompts.
- Development model: experimentation playground, not deterministic Gantt chart with eight-week milestones.

## Hybrid-role advantage
- The people winning are polymaths — designers who can prototype in code, PMs with design sensibility, engineers who think in UX.
- Role boundaries blur; individual attitude and cross-function curiosity matter more than title.
- "Chef just gained access to amazing new ingredients" — the sin is not getting comfortable with them.

## Form factor follows model capability
- GPT-3.5 era → GitHub Copilot (autocomplete a few lines).
- Current models → Cursor Composer, full-app generation, agentic workflows.
- Every model release implies *novel* form factors; you can't ride an old UX and bolt new models underneath.
- Airtable's move: made the agent (Omni) the default way to do everything; the app itself became an artifact the agent manipulates.

## Key Takeaways
- IC CEO: cut standing 1:1s, use AI hourly, be the top inference-cost user in your own product.
- Fast-thinking (weekly jaw-drops) + slow-thinking (deliberate infra) — borrow Kahneman's split for org design.
- Refounding test: if starting today, would you build this? If no, sell and start the next incarnation.
- Chief tastemaker — taste is set by the CEO being in the details, not reading summaries.
- Play, don't check-the-box — explicit permission to cancel a week of meetings and just try AI products.
- Prototypes over decks; development is an experimentation playground, not a Gantt chart.
- Model capability shifts imply new form factors — you can't bolt new models onto old UX.
