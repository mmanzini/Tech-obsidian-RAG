# Elena Verna — The AI-Era Growth Playbook (Lovable)

**Source:** Lenny's Podcast — Head of Growth at Lovable (record 4th appearance)

Elena led growth at Lovable during its run to $200M ARR in under a year with fewer than 100 people. She argues 60-70% of the traditional growth playbook no longer transfers; the job has shifted from optimization to reinvention.

## 95/5 Innovation vs. Optimization

- In prior roles: ~5% of time on innovation, 95% on optimization.
- At Lovable: flipped to **95% innovation, 5% optimization**.
- Category moves too fast to tune the funnel; you must ship new growth loops constantly.
- Growth team launches core product integrations (Shopify, voice mode) — not just onboarding smoothing.

## Activation Is Now the Core Team's Job

- In agent-first products, activation lives inside the agent itself, not onboarding flows.
- Growth stops micro-optimizing activation because the core product team obsesses over the first generation.
- "Aha moment" → **"wow moment"**: first preview after first prompt, not completion.

## Minimum Lovable Product > MVP

- Viability is a 2010s concept; in 2026 the bar is **Minimum Lovable Product**.
- Software cost has collapsed, so invest the saved effort into emotional design.
- Designer should be one of the first hires, not a later addition.
- Best way to fix a bug internally: call it "not lovable" — prioritization is immediate.

## Give the Product Away (Aggressively)

- AI margins are ~40%, not 80-90% — don't treat LLM cost as a line to reduce.
- Freemium is the floor, not the ceiling. Hand out credits to hackathons, events, users who want to evangelize.
- LLM/giveaway spend is logged as **marketing cost, not COGS**.
- Logic: removing monetization friction wins the category before competitors hook the user.

## Product Market Fit Is Perishable

- PMF must be **recaptured every ~3 months** — messaging goes stale in that window.
- Maintain constant shipping noise so social becomes the re-engagement channel (not newsletters).
- Every product engineer doubles as a marketer (announces their own ships).

## Organic = Social, Not SEO

- Five years ago organic meant SEO/Google. Today organic means **X + LinkedIn for B2B**, TikTok/IG for consumer.
- Founder-led socials + employee socials beat branded marketing content.
- Influencer marketing is **~10x larger than paid social** for Lovable.

## Hiring for Chaos

- Screen for **high agency + high autonomy** — people who create clarity out of chaos.
- Paid work trials plus probation periods — the pace isn't for everyone.
- A 10-day vacation feels like a full re-onboarding; the company reinvents weekly.
- Introduced the role of **professional vibe coder** (see [[lennys-podcast/lazar-jovanovic|Lazar Jovanovic]]).

## Key Takeaways

- Flip the ratio: spend 95% inventing new loops, 5% optimizing existing ones.
- Stop micro-optimizing activation; the agent team owns it now.
- MLP (Minimum Lovable Product) replaces MVP — design is a day-one hire.
- Treat LLM costs as marketing, not COGS; give the product away to accelerate word of mouth.
- Product Market Fit decays every ~3 months — plan re-launches, not launches.
- Organic growth in 2026 is founder/employee socials, not SEO.
